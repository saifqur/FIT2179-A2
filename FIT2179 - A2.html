Who Is Carrying the Weight?

Based on the ABS 2022 National Health Survey, nearly two-thirds of Australian adults live with overweight or obesity. Men are more affected than women, and rates climb sharply with age.

Where You Live Matters

Obesity and inactivity rates vary significantly across Australian states and territories. Tasmania and Queensland consistently show the highest rates, while the ACT and Victoria tend to perform better. How Active Are Australians?

Australian guidelines recommend at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week for adults. In 2022, 41% of adults still fell short — a large improvement from 65% in 2017–18, but older Australians and women remain significantly less active.

Obesity — Who Is Most Affected?

Obesity rates climb with age for both men and women. Men have consistently higher rates until their 60s. The long-term trend shows obesity has nearly doubled since 1995, rising from 19% to 32% of Australian adults.
